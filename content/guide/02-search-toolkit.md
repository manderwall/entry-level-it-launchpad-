## How to set up job alerts (2 minutes each)

- **Indeed:** run a search, then click "Get new jobs for this search by
  email" near the top of the results (or toggle Job Alert in the app)
  and set it to daily.
- **LinkedIn:** run a search, toggle "Set alert" at the top of the
  results list, choose Daily, and pick email or app notification.
- **Dice:** run a search, click "Save Search" and turn on email alerts
  in account settings.
- **ZipRecruiter:** run a search, click "Get New Jobs Emailed to You"
  and confirm the email frequency.

Set 2-3 alerts total (one remote, one local/zone-specific, one broad) so
new postings land in your inbox instead of needing a fresh manual search
every day.

## Application scorecard

Use this before applying. Prioritize jobs with 7+ points (see the
interactive version on the Search Toolkit page).

**Apply immediately if:** the role is $19+, entry-level, mentions
technical/customer support, accepts customer service experience, and
does not require 3+ years of IT.

**Skip or deprioritize if:** it pays under $19, is commission-only,
requires unpaid training, or asks you to buy equipment.

## Red flags to avoid

- A "remote IT job" that requires paying for training or equipment upfront
- A company that sends a check for equipment
- Vague job posts with no company name
- "IT" roles that are actually commission-only sales
- $14-$16/hr help desk unless it is short-term with a clear conversion path
- Data-entry jobs claiming unusually high pay with no real interview process
- Interviews conducted only through text/chat with no real company email domain
