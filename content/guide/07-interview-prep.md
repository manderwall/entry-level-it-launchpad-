## 60-second pitch

> "I'm a Per Scholas graduate and CompTIA A+ certified. My background
> includes remote customer service, so I'm comfortable supporting users
> by phone, chat, and email, documenting issues, and staying calm with
> frustrated customers. I'm looking for an entry-level technical support
> or help desk role where I can combine my customer support experience
> with my A+ troubleshooting foundation."

## Interview tip

For every technical question, briefly explain the concept, then connect
it to helping a user. Employers hiring at this level care as much about
communication and process as raw technical depth.

## STAR-format stories from customer service experience

Behavioral questions often start with "Tell me about a time...". Use the
STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result) and pull real examples from
your remote customer service work. Prompts for three common questions —
a difficult customer, solving a problem alone, and learning something
quickly — are on the Interview Prep page. Write out 2-3 of these in full
before interviews, using real details from your own job. A specific,
real story beats a generic answer every time.
