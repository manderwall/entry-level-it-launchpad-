## Practical experience builder: 5 small projects

For someone with A+ but limited work experience, a few documented
projects make the resume much stronger. Put these under a resume section
called **Technical Projects**:

1. **Windows troubleshooting notes** — a one-page guide for five common
   problems (slow computer, no internet, printer, Outlook/email,
   password/login).
2. **Ticketing practice** — simulate tickets in Trello/Notion/a
   spreadsheet with issue, priority, steps, resolution, escalation.
3. **Microsoft 365 basics** — practice Outlook, Teams, OneDrive via free
   Microsoft Learn modules.
4. **Active Directory / user-management lab** — VirtualBox/Hyper-V +
   Windows Server evaluation; create a user, reset a password,
   disable/enable an account, create a group, join a domain.
5. **Basic networking lab** — document your home network: router/modem,
   Wi-Fi name, IP, DNS, DHCP, speed test, troubleshooting steps.

These do not need to be fancy. A simple one-page write-up for each
project is enough. The goal is to show: "I can troubleshoot, document,
and explain what I did."

## Best next certification after A+

Do not pause the job search to study forever. Apply while learning.

1. **Microsoft 365 Fundamentals (MS-900)** — best quick add-on for help desk / office support
2. **Network+** — best for NOC, desktop support, and network support
3. **Security+** — best for SOC, cybersecurity, and federal contractor roles
4. **ITIL Foundation** — helpful for service desk environments, but less urgent than MS-900 or Network+

Cost, prep time, and ROI notes for each (plus bonus AZ-900/MD-102) are in
`data/certs.json` and the interactive calculator on the Projects & Certs
page.
