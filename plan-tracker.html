    If it feels like nothing is working

    Rejections and silence are the normal texture of this process, not a sign you're doing it wrong. Most people apply to dozens of roles before getting an offer — a stretch of "no" or no response at all is expected, not a verdict on you.

    If you're close to quitting for the day (or the week), do this instead of stopping entirely:

    1. Pick the 3 easiest applications you can find right now (roles you're clearly qualified for, simple application forms).
    2. Apply to those 3. Nothing else.
    3. Update your tracker, then stop. That's a real, complete day — not a failure to hit 40-60.

    Rejected applications are not wasted effort — each one is practice, and every "no" gets you closer to a "yes." Update the row's status and move to the next one; there's no need to dwell on any single rejection before continuing.

    If it's been several weeks with real volume (40+ applications) and few or no interviews, that's a signal to adjust the approach, not a signal to stop:

    • Re-score your last 10 applications against the application scorecard — if most scored below 7, you may be applying to roles that are a weak match, not a "you" problem.
    • Widen to stretch/next-step roles for a few weeks — they need more networking or a home-lab project, but they open up more of the market.
    • Ask someone (a Per Scholas career coach, a mentor, a friend in tech) to look at your resume with fresh eyes — it's hard to see what's not landing in your own materials.
    • Try a staffing agency or contract-to-hire pipeline — they often move faster than direct applications and can get you in front of a hiring manager sooner.
    • Double-check you're not missing zones or remote postings you've been skipping out of habit.

    None of this means starting over — it means adjusting targets with everything you've already built (resume, pitch, stories, tracker history) still intact.
